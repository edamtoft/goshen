Quick Facts

1  Leaders Required?

All units must provide adult leadership at camp, unless the youth(s) attending are provisional Boy Scouts. There is no provisional program for Webelos Resident Camps. If a contingent is only a parent/son combination, that is acceptable.

2  Who Registers?

Only one person, the Unit Registrar, should manage the registration, enter information, and make payments on behalf of the unit. (Parents should contact their unit registrar to register their youth to go to camp.)

3  Reserve Your Spot

To reserve your unitâ€™s spot, the unit registrar needs to enter their unit information and pay a non-refundable deposit payment of $360. (This is the cost of just one youth!)

4  Password?

With the new online system, no username or password is required. Everything is handled with the email address you provide.

5  Adding Campers

The registrar does not need to know exactly how many people are coming to reserve space at camp. Additional attendees can be added as youth and adults commit. (The registrar will receive an email that gives further details AFTER the first payment is made.)

6  Free Adult Policy!

Every unit will receive two free adults, regardless of the unit size. The system will automatically subtract the cost when you go to Make a Payment.

7  Pay in Full

Each participant will need to pay in full to be considered registered for Goshen Summer Programs.

8  Camperships

If you plan on applying for a campership for a youth, please DO NOT pay the full amount right away. Voucher codes will be sent out after camperships are awarded.

Camperships

All Campership requests for Goshen Scout Reservation are due at the Council Service Center by February 1 of each year. The Campership Committee will convene and make award designations. Requests received after the due date will only be considered if funds are still available. If you plan on applying for a campership for a youth, please DO NOT pay the full amount right away. Voucher codes will be sent out after camperships are awarded.
